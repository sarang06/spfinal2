From the sage on the stage to the lecture in the pocket

A Theory of Evolution of Education

Posted By QED Technologies on October 10, 2018

Entire world is rapidly getting transformed into a knowledge driven society and the education is also changing with this. Along with time Education evolved from Education 1.0. [Guru-Shishya method of teaching], to Education 2.0 [massification of education with the teacher as sage on the stage and the student as the passive listeners], to Education 3.0 [use of digital technologies and Internet in teaching and learning, which helped increasing access and equity] and finally to Education 4.0 [fueled by high- speed Internet, mobile technology, social media platforms etc. to facilitating personalized learning anytime anywhere and changing the role of teachers to facilitators, guide and mentors].

India and Education 4.0

A young and aspirational India, putting its energy and resources to convert its demographic dividend into economic dividend; and its ease of technology adoption have created this change momentum. Today’s new-age literacy is significantly driven by media, Internet and social media technologies. These new paradigms needs new learn-unlearn-relearn and delivery models for the schools and universities.

Today, the sources of knowledge are global and learning is pervasive; and hence Education 4.0 stresses on preparing students for future leadership positions in a global knowledge society.

Adapt or Perish

The schools and universities need to make a shift away from the process driven, technology supported mass teaching systems to a new way of education delivery that appreciates the personalization of learning. Flexible learning, focus on imparting life skills, student centric learning models and use of technology; and this is the foundation concept of “Education 4.0”. Academic institutions and regulatory framework have to embrace Education 4.0 technologies and processes at a much faster pace than it did more than three decades ago when computer and Internet education came to India.
